Interviews with Liberation War Figures

Listen to the voices of those who played pivotal roles during the Liberation War of Bangladesh or were profoundly affected by its impact. Each interview offers a unique insight into the experiences of individuals during the conflict.

Interview with Major General Shafiullah

Role: Military Commander of Bangladesh Forces

Summary: Major General Shafiullah shares his experiences as one of the leading figures in Bangladesh's military struggle for independence. He discusses key moments of the war, the challenges faced by the freedom fighters, and his reflections on the legacy of the war.

Interview with Begum Rokeya

Role: Civilian Survivor

Summary: Begum Rokeya recounts her experiences as a civilian living through the horrors of the Liberation War. She describes how her community was affected, the losses they endured, and the courage it took to rebuild their lives after the war.

Interview with Dr. Ziauddin Ahmed

Role: Medical Officer during the Liberation War

Summary: Dr. Ziauddin Ahmed, a medical officer during the war, provides insights into the medical challenges faced by the freedom fighters and civilians. He describes the makeshift hospitals, the shortage of supplies, and the resilience of the medical community during the conflict.

Interview with Colonel A.R. Chowdhury

Role: Military Strategist

Summary: Colonel A.R. Chowdhury delves into the military strategies employed by the Bangladesh forces. He highlights key operations, battle tactics, and the coordination between the freedom fighters and allied forces during the Liberation War.
